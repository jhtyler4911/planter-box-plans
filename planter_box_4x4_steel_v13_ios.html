All-steel: No wood means no rot, no CCA concerns, no wood-soil contact issues. All three box types share the same corner post spec (2″×2″×3/16″ angle iron), the same 48″×48″ top cap frame, the same inside-fit bottom rail frame, and the same hardware. Only post length and panel height differ.
Gusset ledge: The tube gusset ledge is always 12″ up from the post bottom on all three box types — this is what establishes the floor at 12″ above grade regardless of box depth.
Panel widths: First pair of panels cut to ≈47⅝″ (48″ minus 2× post wall thickness). Second pair measured and cut to actual gap after first pair is installed. Same process on all three box types.

Bottom Rail

Four pieces per box — one per side. 2″×2″×3/16″ angle iron, cut to 47⅝″ with 45° miters on both ends. Horizontal leg faces down and rests on the tube gusset ledges at 12″ above grade. Vertical leg faces inward and bolts to the interior face of each corner post. No slits — full cross-section intact. All 24 pieces across six boxes are identical.

Fabrication sequence

StepAction
1 — CutCut 4 pieces per box to 47⅝″ with 45° miters on both ends. All 24 pieces are identical — batch all six boxes at once.
2 — Drill bolt holesDrill two ¼″ bolt holes through the vertical leg at each end, centered on the leg, evenly spaced — while pieces are flat on the bench.
3 — Drill panel pilot holesPre-drill 5/32″ pilot holes in the vertical leg face at panel attachment locations — 2–3 holes per piece, evenly spaced — while flat on the bench.
4 — Weld X-ribsClamp the four rail pieces for one box in their final positions. Weld the X-rib assembly to the underside of the horizontal legs. Repeat for all 6 sets.
5 — TreatCold galvanizing compound on all cut ends and weld zones → self-etching primer → enamel topcoat.
6 — Install on siteSet pieces inside the four corner posts, horizontal leg down on gusset ledges. Fasten with ¼″ × ¾″ grade 5 bolts, washers, nylon lock nuts — 2 per end. Level before fully tightening.
Load path: All soil weight transfers from the expanded metal floor → X-ribs → bottom rail horizontal legs → gusset ledges → corner posts → grade. No slits means the full 3/16″ wall carries this load at every end — an important improvement over the previous slit design.
Panel attachment: The corrugated panel bottom edge bears against the vertical leg (inward-facing). Screws go through the panel into the pre-drilled pilot holes in the vertical leg. The panel is captured between the vertical leg and the box interior.

Assembly Order

All cutting is done first — angle iron, flat bar, square tube, corrugated panels. Then all welding and drilling in the shop. Then finishing. Then site installation. This sequence means you never pick up a grinder or drill press mid-assembly.

Phase 1 — All cuts, in shop

01
Cut all 2″×2″×3/16″ angle iron — corner posts & bottom rail pieces (9 sticks)

Follow the verified 9-stick cut plan in the Materials tab, which accounts for 1/8″ kerf per cut. Sticks 1–6: 4×47⅝″ bottom rail pieces + 1×36″ post each. Stick 7: 2×36″ + 6×24″. Stick 8: 7×30″ + 1×24″. Stick 9: 1×30″ + 1×24″. Keep the ~15.5′ remainder from stick 9 as shop stock for gusset cuts in step 03. Bottom rail pieces get 45° miters on both ends. Corner posts are square cut. Mark 12″ floor line on every post. Label post groups: 36″ (Deep), 30″ (Mid), 24″ (Shallow).

02
Cut all 1½″×1½″×⅛″ angle iron — top cap pieces (6 sticks)

4 pieces per box at 48″ with 45° miters on both ends. 24 pieces total across all 6 boxes. Follow the 6-stick cut plan in the Materials tab — 4 pieces per stick, 3 cuts per stick, ~47.6″ remainder per stick kept as shop stock.

03
Cut gusset ledges from 1¾″ square tube — 24 pieces at 2″

Cut from the one 6′ drop (or the stick 9 remainder from step 01 if it's square tube — confirm stock). 24 cuts × 2″ = ~50.9″ consumed including kerf. One gusset per corner post.

04
Cut X-rib flat bars — 1½″×3/16″ flat bar (3 sticks)

5 pieces per stick at 48″, 4 cuts per stick. 12 pieces needed across all 6 boxes, 15 produced — 3 spare. Square cuts, no miter.

05
Cut corrugated steel panels to height

Rip 4×8 sheets to panel height for each box type — 24″ for Deep, 18″ for Mid, 12″ for Shallow — ribs running horizontal. Cut first pair of each box side to ≈47⅝″ wide. Hold second pair uncut until first pair is installed on site and actual gap is measured.

Phase 2 — Shop fabrication: drilling, welding, finishing

06
Prep X-rib flat bars

Grind top corner back 3/16″ for first 2″ on all four ends of every bar. Grind smooth radius on all bottom outer corners. Cut center half-lap notch ¾″ deep on each bar (one from top, one from bottom). Interlock pairs flush, weld half-lap joints. Apply cold galvanizing to all ground and cut areas. Set aside 12 completed X assemblies.

07
Drill and finish bottom rail pieces (all 24)

Drill two ¼″ bolt holes through the vertical leg at each end — 2 holes per end, 4 per piece. Pre-drill 5/32″ pilot holes in the vertical leg face for panel attachment — 2–3 holes per piece, evenly spaced. Weld one X-rib assembly to the underside of each set of four rail pieces (horizontal leg down). Apply cold galvanizing, primer, enamel.

08
Weld gusset ledges to corner posts

Weld one 2″ square tube gusset inside each corner post at exactly 12″ from the post bottom — same position on all three post lengths. Two welds per gusset (base and one side). Confirm gusset top face is level across all four posts for each box set.

09
Drill and finish corner posts (all 24)

Drill ¼″ bolt holes for top cap attachment near the top of each post. Drill ¼″ bolt holes for bottom rail attachment just above each gusset, on the interior face. Pre-drill 5/32″ pilot holes in post leg faces for panel attachment — 2–3 holes per face, aligned to corrugation peaks. Apply cold galvanizing, primer, enamel. Keep groups labeled.

10
Weld and finish top cap frames (all 6)

Assemble 4 mitered 48″ pieces per frame on a flat surface. Tack-weld each inside corner, check square, full fillet weld all four inside corners. Grind exterior miter seams flush. Drill ¼″ bolt holes through vertical leg at each corner for post attachment. Pre-drill 5/32″ pilot holes in vertical leg face for panel attachment — 2–3 holes per side. Apply cold galvanizing, primer, enamel. Cure fully before handling.

Phase 3 — Site installation

11
Set corner posts on site

Position four posts for one box in a 48″×48″ square. Confirm with framing square and tape measure. Shim legs as needed on the concrete patio surface.

12
Install bottom rail pieces

Set each of the four rail pieces inside the corner posts, horizontal leg down, resting on the gusset ledges at 12″ above grade. Fasten with ¼″ × ¾″ grade 5 bolts, washers, nylon lock nuts — 2 per end. Level before fully tightening.

13
Bolt top cap frame

Set cap frame down over posts on the outside. Fasten with ¼″ × ¾″ grade 5 bolts, washers, nylon lock nuts — 2 per corner. Check square and level.

14
Install expanded metal floor

Cut floor sheet to actual interior dimension. Lay on X-ribs and bottom rail horizontal legs. Wire-tie at ribs and corners — ~15 ties per box.

15
Lay landscape fabric on floor

Cut 3-oz fabric to fit interior. Lay on expanded metal, fold edges up against posts ~4″, wire-tie to hold in place.

16
Cut and attach corrugated panels

Install first pair of panels (pre-cut to ≈47⅝″ wide): screw into post faces (2–3 per face), into top cap vertical leg at top, into bottom rail vertical leg at bottom. Measure actual gap for second pair, cut to fit, install. Ribs must run horizontal on all panels.

17
Fill

Fill with growing medium in layers, tamping lightly between layers. Top off after soil settles in first week of watering. Do not fill until box is in permanent position — filled weight is 550–1,300 lbs depending on depth.

Batch efficiency: Complete all Phase 1 cuts for all 6 boxes before starting any welding. Complete all Phase 2 shop work before moving anything to site. This minimizes tool changes and keeps the shop floor clear.
Filled weights: 24″ Deep: ~1,100–1,300 lbs. 18″ Mid: ~820–970 lbs. 12″ Shallow: ~550–640 lbs. Place all boxes in final position before filling — they are not moving after that.
Layout idea: Group the two 24″ deep boxes together for the tomatoes, the two 18″ mid boxes for cucumbers and peppers, the two 12″ shallow boxes where they'll get good light for the greens. Leave ≥24″ between boxes for access.
